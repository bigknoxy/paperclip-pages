WCAG Color Contrast Guidelines

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define specific contrast ratio requirements to ensure text is readable for users with visual impairments. Our color contrast checker helps you meet these standards.

How Contrast Ratio is Calculated

The contrast ratio between two colors is calculated using the relative luminance of each color. The formula is:

Contrast Ratio = (L1 + 0.05) / (L2 + 0.05)

Where L1 is the relative luminance of the lighter color and L2 is the relative luminance of the darker color. Relative luminance is calculated based on the RGB values of the color, weighted for human perception.

Why Accessibility Matters

  • Legal Compliance: Many jurisdictions require WCAG compliance for public websites
  • Better UX: Good contrast benefits all users, especially in bright sunlight
  • SEO Benefits: Accessible sites often rank better in search results
  • Broader Audience: Approximately 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women have color vision deficiency
  • Brand Reputation: Shows commitment to inclusive design

Best Practices

  • Aim for AAA compliance when possible, especially for body text
  • Test your colors in both light and dark modes
  • Don't rely on color alone to convey information
  • Consider users with low vision and color blindness
  • Test with actual users when possible
